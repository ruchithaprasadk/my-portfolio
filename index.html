A · 01
Mall Customer Segmentation Analysis
A complete data analytics project using Excel pivot tables to segment 200 mall customers, uncover spending patterns by age, gender, and income, and deliver retailer-ready recommendations — presented with PowerPoint slides.
Microsoft Excel Pivot Tables COUNTIFS / AVERAGEIFS PowerPoint Data Cleaning Charts & Scatter Plots
⌄
Objective

To analyze a real-world retail customer dataset, identify meaningful behavioral segments across demographic variables, and translate those segments into actionable marketing and engagement recommendations for mall management.

Process
  1. Acquired the Mall Customers dataset (200 records: CustomerID, Gender, Age, Annual Income, Spending Score 1–100) and identified 7 data quality issues in the raw file.
  2. Cleaned the data: standardized inconsistent Gender labels, imputed 2 missing values using column AVERAGE(), removed 1 duplicate record, capped an out-of-range Spending Score (150 → 100), and corrected a negative income value. Documented all steps in a Cleaning Log tab.
  3. Created two derived fields — Age Group (5 buckets: 18–24 through 55+) and Income Bracket (Low / Mid / High / Premium) — to enable richer segmentation.
  4. Built 4 live pivot-style analysis tables using COUNTIFS, AVERAGEIFS, and SUMIFS formulas: by Gender, by Age Group, by Income Bracket, and a cross-tab of Gender × Age Group.
  5. Generated 4 embedded charts (bar charts for age and gender, pie chart for income distribution, scatter plot of Income vs. Spending Score) and documented 5 key insights with 4 strategic recommendations.
  6. Synthesized all findings into a 10-slide PowerPoint presentation with narrative script for a 5–7 minute recorded presentation.
Key Findings

Customers aged 18–34 showed the highest average spending scores (~53–54), while the 55+ group — the largest age segment — scored lowest at 34.4. Annual income and spending behavior showed no strong linear correlation (confirmed by scatter plot). The Premium (100k+) income bracket, though smallest (9% of customers), had the highest average spending score at 48.4.

Artifact Value Proposition

This analysis identifies 3 high-priority action segments (18–34 high spenders, 55+ re-engagement targets, and Premium-income VIPs) from a 200-record dataset — giving mall retailers a data-backed targeting framework that can increase engagement revenue by an estimated 10–15% without additional customer acquisition spend.

A · 02
History & Achievements of Artificial Intelligence
A comprehensive research presentation tracing 70+ years of AI milestones — from Alan Turing's 1950 paper to modern generative AI — designed as an executive-ready reference for teams beginning their AI adoption journey.
PowerPoint Academic Research Claude AI Timeline Visualization Infographic Design
⌄
Objective

To create a structured, visually engaging research presentation that maps the full arc of AI development — covering foundational theory, key breakthroughs, landmark applications, and the emergence of modern generative AI — usable by both technical and non-technical audiences.

Process
  1. Define five eras of AI development: Foundations (1950–1969), Knowledge Systems (1970–1989), AI Winter & Revival (1990–2009), Deep Learning Era (2010–2019), and the Generative AI Age (2020–present).
  2. Researched milestone events per era using academic sources, IEEE publications, and verified AI history timelines. Used Claude AI to validate and cross-reference key dates and attribution.
  3. Identified 20+ landmark achievements including the Turing Test (1950), Perceptron (1958), Expert Systems (1970s), IBM Deep Blue (1997), ImageNet & AlexNet (2012), GPT-3 (2020), and ChatGPT (2022).
  4. Structured the presentation with a visual timeline, era-by-era breakdowns, "why it mattered" commentary for each milestone, and a forward-looking section on AI's trajectory.
  5. Designed slides with consistent visual language: era color-coding, quote callouts from key AI figures, and a visual "AI Impact Wheel" showing sectors transformed by AI.
Key Themes

AI progress has not been linear — the field experienced two major "winters" driven by overinflated expectations. Each revival was triggered not by theory alone, but by increases in compute power and data availability. The 2017 Transformer architecture paper ("Attention Is All You Need") proved to be the single most consequential technical contribution to modern AI.

Artifact Value Proposition

This presentation compresses 70+ years of AI history into a single 20-minute briefing, saving leadership teams an estimated 10–15 hours of independent research when building internal AI literacy programs — and providing a defensible, source-backed narrative for AI strategy discussions.

A · 03
Las Vegas Residential Real Estate Market Report (2026–2027)
A data-driven real estate analysis tracking the transition from the 2026 current baseline into 2027 projections, examining the counter-balancing economic forces of home price inflation and mortgage rate deflation.
Market Analysis Real Estate Economics Predictive Modeling Strategic Planning Academic Research
⌄
Objective

To evaluate the shifting dynamics of the Las Vegas residential housing market, parse complex socioeconomic data down to high-impact variables affecting buyer purchasing power, and provide an actionable, forward-looking framework for strategic planning.

Process
  1. Established a 2026 operational baseline using verified historical data from Las Vegas REALTORS® (LVR) and Freddie Mac, confirming a median single-family home price of $490,000 against a 6.50% 30-year fixed mortgage rate.
  2. Modeled 2027 market projections by correlating macroeconomic variables, factoring in Clark County's net migration of ~42,000 residents annually against a tightening inventory supply projected to drop below 3 months. Using Gemini.
  3. Contextualized historic-high valuations by analyzing the structural reset of the 2020–2022 pandemic boom, demonstrating how a 50% price spike established a permanent floor under local housing inventory.
  4. Mapped geographic submarket divergence across the valley, contrasting hyper-competitive seller markets (Summerlin and Henderson) with affordable starter-home valves (North Las Vegas).
  5. Formatted into an accessible strategic brief complete with executive findings, core data matrices, localized leverage windows, and balanced advice for navigating the market without anticipating a 2008-style crash.
Key Insight

Using AI analysis Studied that: Subsequent interest rate hikes successfully compressed sales volume but completely failed to drop home values because pandemic-era migration patterns anchored local inventory floors. As interest rates dip toward the low 6% range in 2027, expanded purchasing power will pull buyers back into the market—absorbing listings faster than new inventory can arrive and driving home prices up to a projected $500,000–$510,000+.

Artifact Value Proposition

The Las Vegas Residential Real Estate Market Report equips buyers and strategic planners with a clear-eyed predictive framework that highlights a temporary 2026 buyer leverage window—allowing stakeholders to capitalize on seller flexibility before dropping 2027 interest rates reignite intense marketplace competition..
